### 📡 SIGNAL 1: AI-Powered Cyberattacks Are Accelerating

AI-generated phishing emails now have a **54% click-through rate** — compared to 12% for traditional phishing. Attackers are using large language models to craft hyper-personalised messages that bypass spam filters and exploit individual psychology.

Deepfake voice and video attacks hit the enterprise sector hard in Q1 2026, with threat actors cloning C-suite voices to authorise wire transfers. CrowdStrike's latest threat report flags a **300% increase** in AI-augmented social engineering since 2024.

The attacks aren't just smarter — they're autonomous. Agentic AI systems are now scanning networks, identifying vulnerabilities, and exploiting them without human intervention.

### 📡 SIGNAL 2: Agentic AI Opens New Attack Surfaces for Developers

If you're building with AI agents — autonomous systems that use tools, make decisions, and chain actions — you're introducing attack vectors that traditional security models weren't designed for.

The OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications now explicitly covers **prompt injection in agentic workflows**, **excessive tool permissions**, and **data exfiltration through agent memory**. NIST has launched a dedicated AI Agent Standards Initiative to address these risks.

Every developer deploying agentic AI needs a security framework that covers input validation, permission scoping, output filtering, and human-in-the-loop checkpoints. The risk isn't theoretical — it's actively being exploited.

**[Read the full analysis → Agentic AI Security Risks: What Every Developer Must Know](/blog/agentic-ai-security-risks)**

### 📡 SIGNAL 3: Australia Rewrites Its Privacy Laws (and AI Is in the Crosshairs)

Australia's Privacy and Other Legislation Amendment Act 2025 introduces the most significant privacy reforms since the original 1988 act. For the first time, **automated decision-making** using AI systems faces mandatory transparency requirements.

Key changes: a new statutory tort for serious privacy invasions (penalties up to **$50 million** or 30% of annual turnover), a Children's Online Privacy Code, expanded individual rights to erasure and data portability, and mandatory impact assessments for high-risk AI processing.

If you operate in Australia or process data of Australian residents, these reforms demand immediate attention.
